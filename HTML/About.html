COMO SE LLAMA AND THE DREAM OF SUSTAINABLE FASHION

Como Se Llama, an example of how one person's passion can impact the lives of so many others, and change their destinies.

Vanessa and Eric

Vanessa Vargas - Engineer and entrepreneur - and Eric Wou - Programmer and entrepreneur - met in Canada in 2017 and they got married in 2020, and after traveling around the world, they fell in love with the natural landscapes and local fauna of the Andes. However, at the same time, they witnessed social inequality in the treatment of rural communities and the loss of Andean arts due to the imminent exodus to cities.

With the dream of building a sustainable project, in 2022 Vanessa and Eric founded Como Se Llama, a sustainable luxury brand that creates garments from natural fibers of the Andes with the aim of preserving local communities, caring for the environment, and generating local wealth. How? By training artisans and producers, prioritizing sustainable production processes, and, in turn, creating an ethical, sustainable, and transparent value chain.

Thus arises Como Se Llama, a triple-impact company—social, environmental, and economic—that works to generate added value in local communities through fair trade.

Currently, Como Se Llama collaborates with more than 2,500 artisans from different Andean communities, who produce all kinds of garments: sweaters, cardigans, ponchos, shawls, shirts, blankets, scarves, dresses, pants, and more products.

WE RESPECT YOUR SKIN, THE PLANET, AND LOCAL COMMUNITIES

Timeless designs crafted to accompany you through the years. We promote conscious consumption, where fashion becomes a source of well-being for both producers and consumers. We work with natural fibers. Shearing of llamas, vicuñas, and alpacas - which roam freely in the Andes - is done manually, and we prioritize the use of natural colors and dyes as the foundation for all our Collections.

Wearing 'Como Se Llama' is contributing to a positive social impact on cooperatives, producers, and artisans. Be part of the change within the fashion industry, and let's build a more sustainable world together."

OUR MISSION

Revalorizing natural fibers and communities
Celebrating the wisdom of Andean artisans, preserving ancestral skills, providing training, fostering economic development, and promoting fair trade through an ethical value chain.
Moving towards a sustainable fashion paradigm.
Making collaborative efforts to transition towards a more sustainable, ethical, transparent, and inclusive fashion industry. Prioritizing sustainable production processes and respect for the environment.
Promoting responsible consumption.
Advocating for timeless designs meant to last through the years, providing comfort and well-being. Researching current issues, fostering dialogue spaces, and contributing innovative solutions.

THE ROLE OF WOMEN WEAVERS

Did you know that women constitute 85% of the artisan weavers?

The role of women is central to Como Se Llama's sustainable business model. Through the proceeds derived from their labor, these women have not only been able to sustain their households, establish bank accounts, and access healthcare services, but have also autonomously elevated their self-esteem and confidence.

This imparts a positive influence on communities through a viable and respectful trade paradigm that upholds their cultural traditions and imparts their time-honored skills to the realm of fashion.

It is for this reason that Como Se Llama advocates for societal awareness regarding sustainable development, fostering conscientious consumption, and amplifying the global demand for ethical fashion.

Thank you for sharing your skills and wisdom
